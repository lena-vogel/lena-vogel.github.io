by Kenji Tetard, Aymeric Bacuet, Naël Dillenbourg, François Dumoncel, Lena Vogel

Actors and actresses

Through the feminist struggles and the recent advances in gender equality, women have gained space and importance in movies.
Indeed, the cinematic world has long been a mirror reflecting societal norms and changes, and nowhere is this more evident than in the representation of women.
This year, among the 21 movies competing for the Cannes festival, 7 were directed by women.
Moreover, it is easy to see that movies, TV shows and other forms of art talk more and more about women realities, letting the archaic male-gaze in the past.
In this study, we will try and understand gendered dynamics that could involved in the film industry. To do so, we will be using the CMU Movie Corpus dataset. It contains around 80'000 movies released between 1914 and 2012. Here is a graph to show you the evolution of the number of movies released per year!
See? There are, of course very few movies before 1980, this is why to make future graphs which represent values per year simpler to viually analyze, we will restrain the years range to the 30 years between 1980 and 2010 only.

First, we could ask ourselves the following question: "Are there more women actresses who play characters in movies now than before?"
That is interesting! From 1980 to 2010, the proportion of women actresses compared to men increased of more than 7%! It is not a huge increase, but it still is considerable. We'll have to investigate more on that and the potential causes for this increase, as well as whether when women are more represented, it also imply they are better represented.

Potential causes

Now, let us explore the potential causes for the increase of women acrtesses in movies. Here we will only take the data on the movies we have access to. We will not be correlating the growing number of actresses to historical events whatsoever.
The first thing that should come into your mind is: "What about the movie directors and producers?" Lucky you, the next graphs are about the gender proportions of the latter.

Bechdel test

In order to analyze the quality of the increase in another way, we used the data from the Bechdel test (see website here). The Bechdel was originally invented in 1985 in a comic strip by Alison Bechdel and tests whether a movie passes the 3 following conditions: a) The movie has to have at least two women in it, b) who talk to each other, c) about something other than a man. Here is the original illustration:

This results in a score between 0 and 3, depending on the number of tests the movie passes. It is considered to be a basic standard for minimal women's representation in movies and also includes some women's theorized stereotypes such as the Smurfette principle, which designs the fact that in many stories, the woman is the exception and exists only in reference to the men. Even though this test is very limited, it offers great insight on the role and representation of the women in any movie.
Thus, we merged our data with the Bechdel test data (which is entered by hand, where movies are evaluated by individuals) and we plotted the evolution of the Bechdel score over the years.
